# About John G. Tittle

John G. Tittle started on this journey shortly after coming to the Lord on June 14, 1982. He began journaling soon afterwards, mostly in fits and starts, capturing thoughts and observations about the spiritual life.

Looking back over his journals, he can see a progression — the footprints of God in his life, even when he could not see it in the moment. He started to store away observations about how the spiritual world works, noticing patterns and relationships that seemed to follow discoverable laws.

As he writes in the preface: "I am in my 70th year, or as a friend said, since 7 is a perfect number, I am working on being perfect times ten." The energy that keeps him moving is the conviction that the journey itself is an exciting process of discovery.

His professional background includes work at CSC (now DXC Technology), where he developed the discipline of making serious things happen under pressure — a skill he brings to this theological investigation. He borrows the concept of actions echoing in eternity from Marcus Aurelius, while departing sharply from the Stoic framework: for John, the eternity in which actions echo is a living, personal God, not an impersonal cosmos.

This work is dedicated to his kids and grandkids, written as a legacy of one man's honest exploration of how the spiritual world operates.
