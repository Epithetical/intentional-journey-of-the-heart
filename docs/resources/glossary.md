# Glossary of Key Terms

Terms are defined as used in this work, drawing from John's explorations across all five volumes.

**Faith (Pistis/Trust)** — The Greek *pistis*, defined throughout as trust rather than mere intellectual belief. The output of the hearing chain in Romans 10:17: Word → Hearing → Faith.

**Hearing** — Not passive reception but genuine, attentive, willing-to-obey engagement with the Word. The conversion mechanism that transforms the Word of Christ into faith.

**Spiritual Force** — An analogy to physical force, explored in Volume 3. The capacity to produce spiritual change. Faith, authority alignment, and prayer resonance all contribute to spiritual force.

**Spiritual Authority** — The force multiplier explored in Volume 1, Exploration 7. Authority alignment (as demonstrated by the Centurion in Matthew 8) dramatically amplifies the effectiveness of faith.

**Resonance** — The mechanism by which prayer operates, explored in Volume 1, Exploration 8. Persistent, aligned prayer accumulates like resonance in a physical system, reaching threshold points where breakthrough occurs.

**Knots (Emotional Blockages)** — Volume 2's diagnostic framework identifies four primary types of emotional blockages that impede spiritual growth: Grief Knots, Shame Knots, Fear Knots, and Anger Knots. Each has a distinct clearing mechanism.

**The False Self** — The identity structure built around wounds, explored in Volume 2, Exploration 2A. Distinct from the true self that God speaks to, the false self is a defensive construction that blocks genuine hearing.

**The Four Connects** — A causal sequence in Volume 2, Exploration 5: Connecting with Self → Connecting with Others → Connecting with God → Connecting with Mission. Not a workshop label but a logical architecture.

**Container** — The set of conditions required for genuine hearing in community, explored in Volume 2, Exploration 8. Four conditions: Safe, Present, Clear, and Intentional.

**TFT (Transcendental Field Theory)** — The framework adopted in Volume 3 for modeling spiritual dynamics quantitatively. Maps spiritual phenomena onto field-theoretic concepts while maintaining the critical distinction between analogy and identity.

**AQAL (All Quadrants, All Levels)** — Ken Wilber's integral framework, discussed in Volume 1 as a potential meta-framework for organizing the explorations. John engages with it critically, adopting some structural insights while departing on theological grounds.

**The Glory Attractor** — Volume 3's culminating concept: glory as the fixed point of the entire spiritual system, the attractor state toward which sanctification trajectories converge.

**Conservation Laws (Spiritual)** — Volume 3, Exploration 6 investigates whether spiritual quantities are conserved. Key finding: spiritual capacity is *not* conserved when unused (Parable of the Talents), abundance *exceeds* conservation (Loaves and Fishes), and blocked capacity *can be restored* (Confession-Restoration pair).

**The Miracle Frame** — Volume 1's opening exploration: the natural world is a subset of the spiritual world, not the other way around. Miracles are not violations of natural law but operations of a larger system that includes the natural.

**Taxonomy** — The classification system for laws that evolves across volumes. See [Taxonomy Overview](taxonomy-overview.md) for the full system.
