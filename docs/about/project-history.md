# Project History

**October 5, 2014** — John writes in his journal: "I have been nudged by the Spirit to get my thoughts out in a way that others can understand on this subject." He and his daughter Amanda had a fortuitous lunch where he asked her to consider helping him publish this work. She rightly responded that she would not know how to begin but encouraged him to start writing.

One of the core challenges was simply getting started — the feeling of a huge mountain of detailed work ahead. But the journey itself became the point.

**1982–2014** — Decades of journaling, observation, and study. John accumulated reflections on the patterns and laws governing the spiritual life, drawing from scripture, personal experience, scholarly sources, and community practice.

**2014–2025** — The five volumes took shape over years of writing, revision, and deepening exploration. Different parts were developed at different times, and the work evolved as new insights emerged. The taxonomy system grew from simple structural/operational categories in Volume 1 to include diagnostic, developmental, and field/quantitative classifications by Volume 3.

**2026** — The work reached its v5.3 revision. With the help of Claude as an AI research assistant (documented in the Introduction), the volumes were consolidated, cross-referenced, and prepared for open-source publication.

**March 2026** — This site launched as an open-source theological library, making the work freely available for reading, discussion, and community contribution.
