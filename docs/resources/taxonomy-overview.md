# Taxonomy Overview

The explorations across all five volumes are classified using a taxonomy that evolves as the work deepens. Each law or relationship is tagged with one or more types.

## Structural Laws
*Introduced in Volume 1*

Laws that describe the architecture of spiritual reality — how things are built and related. These are the "what is" laws. Examples: the structure of spirit/heart/soul/body, the relationship of faith/hope/love, the hierarchy of spiritual authority.

## Operational Laws
*Introduced in Volume 1*

Laws that describe how spiritual processes work — the causal chains and mechanisms. These are the "how it works" laws. Example: Romans 10:17 chain (Word → Hearing → Faith).

## Mixed Laws (Structural/Operational)
*Introduced in Volume 1*

Laws that have both structural and operational dimensions. Example: Spiritual Force, Energy, and Power — structural in definition, operational in application.

## Gateway Laws
*Introduced in Volume 1*

Laws that function as preconditions for other laws to operate. The Fear of the Lord is the primary gateway: without it, the operational laws do not engage fully. Like a bias voltage in electronics — not the signal itself, but the condition that allows the signal to flow.

## Diagnostic Laws
*Introduced in Volume 2*

Laws that identify blockages and conditions in the spiritual life. The Heart Soil diagnostic (from the Sower parable) and the Emotional Knots framework are diagnostic — they tell you where the problems are, not how to fix them.

## Tool-Application Laws
*Introduced in Volume 2*

Laws that match specific tools to specific blockages. The Tool Map in Volume 2 Exploration 6 is the primary example: grief knots require naming and completing, shame knots require recovering the gold, fear knots require somatic entry, anger knots require naming before releasing.

## Developmental Laws
*Introduced in Volume 2*

Laws governing growth trajectories and training processes. The Training Plan in Volume 2 and the affective taxonomy in the Formation Documents are developmental — they describe how capacity builds over time.

## Field/Quantitative Laws
*Introduced in Volume 3*

Laws modeled using quantitative frameworks from physics and systems dynamics. Spiritual force, distance, energy, power, and conservation laws are all field/quantitative types. These are the most speculative and least certain.

## Eschatological Laws
*Introduced in Volume 3*

Laws governing the end-state: the Glory Attractor and the Sanctification Trajectory. These describe where the system is heading, not just how it operates in the present.
