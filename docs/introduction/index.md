Preface
Oct 5, 2014:

 I have been "nudged" by the Spirit to get my thoughts out in a way that others can understand on this subject.  I have been remiss in taking what is a collection of thoughts and notes from various situations in my life and trying to organize them in a comprehensible way.

Amanda and I had a fortuitous lunch last week, and I asked her to consider helping me publish this.  She rightly responded that she would not know how to get started, and that I should get someone who knew the steps to at least get one of the four books I have in various stages of draft. She could shadow the process and maybe help with the second one.

One of the points in our discussion was about my challenge to getting started; besides the feeling of this huge mountain of detailed work, editing, and re-editing what I wrote, I was not even sure who I was "talking" to in the process.  Kind of a key point because who I am speaking to makes all the difference in what comes together as I think/talk about things.  My reflection on this key point leads me to try this by "talking" to my kids, the most important audience I have ever had, and the ones I would most love to pass all these reflections on to. So, here goes.
As I start writing all this out, I am in my 70th year, or as a friend said, since 7 is a perfect number, I am working on being perfect times ten. :-)
Cool thought and one of the core energies that keeps me moving along, is that I'm on a journey, and the journey in and of itself is an exciting process that I can wake up excited to get into the day before me, even when I have nothing of significance on my calendar for the day.  But then again, that is never true because I have this deep sense that everyday actions and words are part of a whole.  Every day, even when I can't see it, there is a substrate of awareness that everything I do, as Marcus Aurelius says, "Echoes in eternity". 
I borrow this phrase from Marcus Aurelius, but I want to be clear about where I depart from him. The Stoic eternity in which Aurelius’s actions echo is impersonal — a cosmic order that registers your deeds but knows no names. The eternity I am speaking of is different: it is the eternity of a Person who is listening, who is moved, who remembers, and who will one day receive an account not just of what was done but of who was becoming. C.S. Lewis notes this distinction when he separates the philosophical Absolute from the personal God of Christian revelation. The echoes I am concerned with are not absorbed into an impersonal logos — they are received by a Father who has been paying attention all along. That changes the stakes considerably.

Lots of ways this shows up in my life. I remember a place in my work at CSC where I was just underwater with the details of making serious things happen; the quotidian details of my job were overwhelming at that point in time.  In the middle of all that, in my morning quiet time, I was sort of complaining to the Lord about the drudgery of such a job.  I had a flash vision of me sitting at my desk, typing away at my email and having to deal with some frustrating situation where I was tempted to use words like "You idiot!" in my response to their email.  The vision was me sitting there typing away, but superimposed was me standing, garbed in priestly clothes, arms stretched out above my head, even with a priestly headpiece, worshiping the Lord, at the same time.  I was struck by the awareness that my work was worship, and what I did and how I did it, no matter how mundane, was always holy unto the Lord.  That was a very new thought to me, and has carried forward into many places in my life.

I note that this subject has been explored for centuries, and perhaps even more recently. The very first scientists were almost universal in thinking that by discovering the natural laws, they were getting closer to understanding God and His will. What I have in mind is more than what I see in my research. I want to draw parallels from natural laws and extend them to spiritual laws, including identifying elemental things and quantifying them where possible.  Once identified, I want to describe the relationship and causal statements. I then want to quantify the elements and identify the equations that define the relationships and how they interact with each other.  From that, I want to identify the various conservation laws and field equations.  I describe this as a calculus of the Spirit. This is all a larger goal than may even be attainable, but the journey in and of itself has been really great so far and is exciting for me as I think about what’s next.

Part of the motivation behind this exploration is what I see in scripture, a life where miracles are normal - even expected part of life in Christ.  When Jesus went back to his hometown, he could only “...laid His hands on a few sick people and healed them.” (Mark 6:5) It strikes me that we live so far below the life Jesus offered us.  Part of my encouragement in this work is that we might be able to live a life closer to what I see in scripture. That’s pretty exciting.

So, let me give my reflections as I go on this journey, and this all comes with a personal awareness that every word, every thought, is something that "echoes in eternity" in ways I cannot understand, but forms this core substrate in my awareness.  I don't know what you kids will do with this, if anything, but at least I am being obedient and getting started in documenting my reflections and what I believe the Lord has given me up to this point.  This is part of my process of becoming "ten times perfect".  Sounds almost arrogant, but much of what follows may sound such, because I am going to write out in raw form the way I think about things, and how things fit together for me.  

I also note that I started with the thought that I would discover universal laws that anyone could step into and apply in their lives.  I still think that is true, but it became obvious that this only works by faith and personal revelation.  So this really is a documentation of my personal journey and what I believe the Lord has revealed to me so far.  I think anyone who is willing to take such a journey of faith will make similar if not identical discoveries.

Self-reflective by its very nature, and therefore arrogant sounding for some parts, but this is intended to be me, mostly unedited, and therefore will come across as such. The writing has been an interesting journey in and of itself.  I'm excited that just this very thought has me moving ahead.

No intent to make this an autobiography, but much of that will show up because this is a personal journey I am on.  Mostly about reflections on where my thinking on this subject is now. In this very personal journey, I am inviting you along for the discovery of it all.  Also, no expectations of what you kids might do or respond, that’s up to you.  I’m just trying to be obedient and move forward at this wonder-filled point in my life.

I’m looking forward to this part of my journey and sharing it with you. 

My Blessings,
Daddy and Granddaddy

I started on this journey shortly after coming to the Lord on June 14th, 1982. I started journaling soon afterwards, mostly in fits and starts, just capturing my thoughts and difficulties amid daily life with a job and family. Plenty of pivotal moments are captured in these journals, sometimes recognized but often not seen until later. Carolyn and I, for instance, went through Inner Healing training with Rita Bennett and LeAnn Payne in the mid-80s, which launched us into praying for results with people. We quickly joined a small-group Bible study, and that has continued to shape my understanding of the critical importance of community in spiritual formation. As the Air Force moved us around the country, we got to sample lots of various contexts of worship and church. In a small group setting of a startup Vineyard Church in Oklahoma City we learned how to pray for people while keeping our eyes open! What a surprise. 

Looking back over my journals, I can see a progression, the footprints of God in my life, even when I could not see it in the moment. I started to stop and review these occasionally and ask the Lord where he was taking the family and me. In the 90’s, I started writing down some of the patterns I saw, and eventually, in 2002, I started with a small group of guys to explore what I now call the Intentional Journey of the Heart, with a focus on developing the Laws of the Spirit.
