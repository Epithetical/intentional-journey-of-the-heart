# Reading Guide

This library contains five volumes, three formation documents, and supporting resources. Here are suggested paths depending on your background and interests.

## New Readers
Start with the **[Introduction](introduction/)**, which lays out the four foundational principles and gives an overview of the entire work. Then read **Volume 1** sequentially — the eight explorations build on each other, culminating in "Connecting the Dots" which shows how the pieces fit together.

## Scholars and Researchers
Begin with **[Volume 3: Quantitative Framework](volume-3-quantitative-framework/)** if you're interested in the analytical modeling, and **[Volume 4: Testing Framework](volume-4-testing-framework/)** for the epistemological foundations and testing protocols. Then read Volume 1 for the underlying laws being modeled.

## Pastors and Practitioners
Start with **[Volume 2: Knowing to Doing](volume-2-knowing-to-doing/)** — it covers the practical application: diagnostic tools, emotional blockage clearing, community formation, and a concrete training plan. The **[Formation Documents](formation-documents/)** are also directly applicable to small group ministry.

## Quick Overview
Read the **[Introduction](introduction/)**, then jump to **[Connecting the Dots](volume-1-laws-of-the-spirit/connecting-the-dots/)** in Volume 1 for a summary of how the eight explorations fit together. The **[Master Law Index](volume-3-quantitative-framework/master-law-index/)** in Volume 3 provides a comprehensive cross-reference.

## By Topic
Use the **search bar** (top of page) to find specific concepts across all volumes. The **[Glossary](resources/glossary/)** defines key terms, and the **[Taxonomy Overview](resources/taxonomy-overview/)** explains how laws are classified.
